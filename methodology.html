01Purpose & scope

SENTINEL NOVA is an open-source intelligence (OSINT) aggregation and structured-analysis platform for tracking global security and geopolitical risk across ten operational domains: armed conflict, cyber & infrastructure, terrorism, political risk, climate & disasters, health security, geoeconomics, energy & maritime, WMD / nuclear, and air & travel.

The platform is designed to produce leading-indicator warning rather than lagging situational awareness — it is explicitly built to avoid target fixation on any single theater and to detect weak signals in the analytical margins that conventional region-focused products often miss.

This document establishes the analytical standards, methodology, and tradecraft the platform adheres to. It is written to be understood by non-intelligence-community audiences while remaining rigorous enough to withstand peer review by professional analysts.

Intended audience. Corporate security leaders, geopolitical risk professionals, crisis managers, policy researchers, and informed generalists. Not a substitute for classified intelligence products.

02Analytic frameworks

SENTINEL NOVA combines four established analytic frameworks:

  1. ICD 203 — Analytic Standards (Office of the Director of National Intelligence). Governs probabilistic language, sourcing, alternative analysis, and distinction between facts and judgments.
  2. Admiralty Code (NATO STANAG 2022). Governs source reliability (A–F) and information credibility (1–6) grading.
  3. Indicators & Warnings (I&W). A classical intelligence tradecraft for defining falsifiable hypotheses with specific, observable tripwires.
  4. Structured Analytic Techniques (SATs) as documented by Heuer & Pherson — particularly Analysis of Competing Hypotheses, Key Assumptions Check, Premortem, and Indicator Generation.

Together these frameworks impose three discipline requirements on every assessment: (1) judgments must be expressed in defined probabilistic language, (2) sources must be graded, and (3) claims must be tied back to specific observable indicators.

03ICD 203 confidence language

All forward-looking judgments use the ODNI's standard probabilistic terms. The ranges are taken from the ODNI ICD 203 standard and match the language used in National Intelligence Estimates (NIEs).

TermProbabilityWhen used
ALMOST CERTAIN95–99%Overwhelming evidence across multiple independent, reliable sources; alternative hypotheses effectively eliminated.
HIGHLY LIKELY80–95%Strong convergent evidence; minor uncertainties remain but dominant hypothesis is clearly preferred.
LIKELY55–80%Balance of evidence favors the hypothesis; viable alternatives exist but are less well-supported.
EVEN CHANCE45–55%Evidence is genuinely mixed; the analyst cannot express meaningful preference.
UNLIKELY20–45%Evidence weighs against the hypothesis, but it cannot be ruled out.
VERY UNLIKELY5–20%Strong contrary evidence; hypothesis requires unusual assumptions to hold.
REMOTE1–5%Near-elimination based on all available evidence; retained only for completeness.
Forbidden language. Terms such as "could", "may", "possibly", or "cannot be ruled out" are prohibited as standalone confidence claims. Every probabilistic statement must map to one of the seven bands above.

05Indicators & Warnings methodology

I&W is the structured approach of defining falsifiable hypotheses up-front — before evidence accumulates — then specifying the observable indicators that would, if detected, shift the probability of each hypothesis. Every active watch scenario on the dashboard follows this template.

Design principles

  1. Hypothesis first. The scenario hypothesis is declared before any evidence is considered; this prevents confirmation bias.
  2. Observable tripwires. Each indicator must be an observable event an outside analyst could independently verify from open sources, not an interpretive judgment.
  3. Falsifiability. Tripwires must be written such that their absence over time is itself informative (i.e. green tripwires matter as much as red ones).
  4. Leading, not lagging. Tripwires should fire before the underlying escalation is consummated — the goal is to warn, not to record.
  5. Independence. Tripwires within a scenario should correlate with the hypothesis but be operationally independent of each other, so their joint firing is informative.

Escalation bands per scenario

Tripwires litStatusAnalytic posture
0 of 5GREENScenario at baseline; continue passive monitoring.
1 of 5WATCHActive monitoring; review collection for corroborating signals.
2–3 of 5ELEVATEDRaise scenario to Key Judgment; brief stakeholders.
4+ of 5CRITICALScenario treated as operationally probable; escalate reporting cadence.

06Watch scenarios & tripwires

SENTINEL NOVA currently tracks five primary watch scenarios with five tripwires each (25 indicators total). The scenario set is reviewed quarterly and updated as the global threat picture evolves.

S1 — NATO–Russia escalation (Europe)

Hypothesis. Near-term (0–90 day) escalation of the Russia–NATO posture escalates beyond current Ukraine-centric kinetic activity, with direct Russia–NATO friction at the operational level.

Key assumptions. Russia retains coherent escalation-management control; NATO alliance cohesion holds on Article-level matters; Ukraine remains the primary theater.

T1.1Russian air or naval incursion into NATO territory — deliberate or repeated unintentional crossings trigger Article 4 consultations.
T1.2Undersea cable or pipeline sabotage — attributed or plausibly attributed to Russian state or proxy action (Baltic Sea data cables, Nord Stream-class events, subsea telecom links).
T1.3Russian nuclear or tactical-weapons rhetoric — explicit threats from Moscow referencing tactical-nuclear use, Belarus deployment, or exercise announcements.
T1.4NATO Article 4 or Article 5 consultation — any formal invocation by a member state.
T1.5Kaliningrad / Suwałki Gap forward posture — forward troop, missile, or naval deployment indicative of land-corridor operations.

S2 — PRC coercion of Taiwan (Indo-Pacific)

Hypothesis. PRC adopts coercive posture short of invasion — quarantine, blockade drill, customs inspection regime, or cyber-kinetic infrastructure targeting.

Key assumptions. PRC retains preference for grey-zone coercion; US maintains deterrent posture; Taiwan political leadership does not preemptively escalate.

T2.1PLA exercise or blockade drill around Taiwan — multi-day, multi-service operations with live-fire components.
T2.2Median-line / ADIZ incursions at pattern-change scale — sustained multi-sortie-per-day cadence over a week.
T2.3PRC customs / quarantine inspection regime on Taiwan shipping — formalised interdiction, not one-off incidents.
T2.4Cyber attack on Taiwan critical infrastructure — grid, telecom, financial, or port systems, attributable to PRC state actors (Volt Typhoon, Salt Typhoon patterns).
T2.5US carrier strike group Western Pacific surge — surge deployment or dual-CSG presence indicative of deterrent signalling.

S3 — Iran nuclear breakout (MENA)

Hypothesis. Iran crosses operationally significant nuclear thresholds indicating intent to weaponise rather than hedge.

Key assumptions. IAEA retains some degree of access until active breakout; Israeli strike posture is latent but responsive; sanctions regime continues.

T3.1IAEA loss of access or monitoring — camera removal, inspector expulsion, or suspension of safeguards reporting.
T3.2Enrichment approaching weapons-grade — confirmed enrichment above 60% U-235, particularly movement toward 84–90%.
T3.3Weaponisation or warhead-design activity — test signatures at Parchin-class facilities, implosion-lens research.
T3.4Iranian NPT withdrawal signals — formal diplomatic signalling of Non-Proliferation Treaty exit.
T3.5Fordow or Natanz strike-preparation indicators — defensive hardening, dispersal, or Israeli/US strike-posture build-up.

S4 — Critical infrastructure attack (Western)

Hypothesis. State or state-tolerated actors execute a cyber, physical, or cyber-kinetic attack on Western critical infrastructure at a scale that materially degrades services.

Key assumptions. Below-threshold cyber activity continues routinely; threshold crossing is the analytical focus; attribution is imperfect but improvable.

T4.1Ransomware on utility, hospital, or logistics target — confirmed event with service-degradation impact, not just data theft.
T4.2GPS / PNT jamming or spoofing in civil airspace — sustained, geographically-bounded interference affecting airlines or shipping.
T4.3Power grid SCADA / ICS intrusion — intrusion with demonstrated manipulation capability, not reconnaissance only.
T4.4Financial system cyber incident — bank, SWIFT, clearing, or exchange outage with attribution indicators.
T4.5Port or maritime-logistics disruption — cyber, kinetic, or chokepoint (Panama, Suez) event degrading throughput materially.

S5 — DPRK provocation (Korean Peninsula)

Hypothesis. DPRK conducts a provocation at strategic rather than tactical level, shifting the deterrent balance on the peninsula.

Key assumptions. DPRK retains coherent central control; PRC tolerates rather than encourages escalation; Russia–DPRK cooperation continues.

T5.1ICBM test with successful re-entry — demonstrated re-entry vehicle survival, not just launch.
T5.27th nuclear test — activity at Punggye-ri or equivalent site.
T5.3SLBM or solid-fuel launch — second-strike or mobility indicators.
T5.4Russia–DPRK munitions or technology transfer — large-scale artillery, missile, or fissile-technology movement.
T5.5Cross-DMZ kinetic exchange — artillery, direct fire, or casualty incidents.

Collection architecture

RSS is fetched via a multi-proxy CORS chain (rss2json, allorigins, corsproxy.io, corsproxy.org, codetabs, thingproxy) with automatic fall-through. If every proxy fails, the platform degrades to cached fallback data rather than producing an empty feed, and flags the degraded state in the UI status chip.

GDELT queries use the public doc/doc endpoint with structured query strings composed of theme tokens, geographic filters, and temporal windows.

08Processing & classification

Each event is normalised into a common record: {region, type, sev, text, ts, source}.

Regional taxonomy

Events are tagged with one of six regions: Europe, M.East, Asia-Pacific, Americas, Africa, Global. Regional assignment is regex-based against headline text and falls back to "Global" when no specific region dominates.

Domain taxonomy

Events are classified across ten operational domains: MILITARY, NUCLEAR, CYBER, TERROR, POLITICAL, CLIMATE, HEALTH, ECONOMY (sanctions, tariffs, supply-chain), ENERGY, and AIRSPACE / TRAVEL / SHIPPING. Classification is performed by the classifyIran() function (name retained for code-stability reasons; behaviour is global).

Severity assignment

Severity is assigned at the classification step from a four-tier ladder: LOW MEDIUM HIGH CRITICAL. Assignment uses keyword-weighted heuristics; high-severity keywords include "strike", "breach", "outage", "escalation", "withdrawal", "test"; critical keywords include "nuclear test", "attack", "war", "invasion", "breakout".

Deduplication

Events with near-identical headlines (Levenshtein-style overlap) within a 24-item rolling window are coalesced to their highest-severity instance.

09Severity & escalation scoring

The dashboard publishes a composite Escalation Index capped at 100. The formula is transparent and uses only data visible on the dashboard:

ESCALATION_INDEX = min(100, crit_events × 15 + high_events × 8 + tracked_events × 2)

Interpretation bands

ScoreBandMeaning
0–19BASELINERoutine global tempo; no unusual concentration of high-severity events.
20–39ELEVATEDMeaningful clustering of high-severity events; raise scan frequency.
40–69HIGHMultiple critical events or one dominant crisis; brief stakeholders.
70–100CRITICALStrategic-level event tempo; major decision support required.
Caveat. The Escalation Index is a communications device, not a forecasting model. It aggregates what has already happened; it is not probability of future escalation. Tripwire activation (Section 05) is the forward-looking instrument.

10Key Judgment derivation

Key Judgments (KJs) displayed on the dashboard are auto-derived from watch-scenario tripwire states, then expressed in ICD 203 language. The derivation rules are:

  1. For each scenario, count lit tripwires n out of total t.
  2. Scenarios with n ≥ 1 qualify for KJ generation.
  3. Qualified scenarios are ranked by n descending; the top four become KJs.
  4. Confidence is mapped: n=1→UNLIKELY, n=2→EVEN CHANCE, n=3→LIKELY, n=4+→HIGHLY LIKELY.
  5. KJ text links the confidence band to the scenario name and the specific tripwire count; no judgment is made without a tripwire tie-back.
Transparency principle. Every Key Judgment on the dashboard is traceable to specific, named tripwires. If a KJ cannot be traced to observable indicators, it does not appear.

11Structured Analytic Techniques

Analytical products produced from SENTINEL NOVA should apply the following SATs as appropriate to the question. These are the techniques codified in Heuer & Pherson and adopted across the US IC since the 2005 WMD Commission.

TechniqueUse case
Key Assumptions CheckMake explicit the assumptions underpinning a judgment; test each against disconfirming evidence.
Analysis of Competing Hypotheses (ACH)Weigh evidence against multiple hypotheses rather than seeking confirmation of one. Required for any high-confidence judgment on an actor's intent.
Indicator GenerationThe process by which tripwires in Section 06 are defined — ex-ante declaration of observable signals.
Premortem AnalysisImagine the forecast has already failed; identify why. Required before publishing any CRITICAL-band judgment.
Red Team / Devil's AdvocacyDeliberately construct the strongest contrary case. Applied quarterly to each watch scenario.
What-If AnalysisRelax a key assumption and trace implications. Used when scenario tripwires cluster unexpectedly.

12Cognitive biases & countermeasures

Intelligence analysis is defeated more often by cognitive bias than by missing information. The platform and its operator actively guard against:

BiasRisk in this contextCountermeasure
Target fixationOver-weighting the current dominant crisis (e.g. Ukraine or Iran) while missing margin signals.Ten-domain taxonomy; regional balance in watch scenarios; explicit "Africa" and "Americas" trip categories.
Confirmation biasTripwires are interpreted to confirm prior beliefs.Tripwires are defined ex-ante with regex matchers; analyst cannot retroactively re-specify.
Recency biasThe most recent event dominates analysis.24-item rolling window with severity weighting; single event cannot trip more than one tripwire.
AnchoringInitial severity assessment sticks despite new evidence.Severity reassigned on every classification run; no persistent severity state.
Availability heuristicLoud news drowns out quiet-but-significant signals.GDELT volume normalisation; tracked-event counter independent of news ticker.
Mirror imagingAssuming adversary actors reason as Western actors do.Scenario key-assumptions sections are adversary-specific; tripwires are behavioural, not motive-based.
GroupthinkSingle-analyst product lacks challenge.Mandatory quarterly Red-Team pass; peer review of any CRITICAL-band judgment before publication.

13Limitations & caveats

OSINT only. SENTINEL NOVA consumes exclusively open sources. It has no access to classified, proprietary, or paywalled-premium intelligence streams. Judgments can be improved materially by classified products and should not be taken as a substitute for them.
Regex-based tripwires. Current tripwire detection is pattern-matching against event text. This is fast and transparent but will produce false negatives (a real event described in unexpected language) and false positives (headlines that use watch terms in unrelated ways). A human review step is required before acting on any CRITICAL-band judgment.
Source latency. Public RSS and GDELT feeds are behind the underlying event by minutes to hours. For real-time operational decisions, classified feeds or commercial tactical sources are required.
Rolling-window truncation. The platform retains roughly the latest 24 tracked events per stream. Historical pattern analysis is not supported by this version.
No attribution layer. Events are classified by domain and severity but not by responsible actor. Attribution remains analyst judgment.
Proxy dependency. Collection depends on public CORS proxies that can rate-limit, degrade, or disappear. A production deployment with authenticated API access would be materially more robust.

15Governance & review

  • Analytical owner. Single-analyst product; all judgments attributable to the named analyst in the header metadata.
  • Peer review. Any CRITICAL-band Key Judgment should receive at least one peer review before external distribution.
  • Change log. Scenario additions, deletions, or substantive modifications are logged in the repository commit history with rationale.
  • Dissenting views. Where the analyst holds a meaningfully different view from the auto-derived Key Judgment, that view is recorded as a footnote in the exported Word product.
  • Retrospective analysis. Quarterly review of Key Judgments against subsequent ground truth; calibration drift is corrected in the confidence mapping.
